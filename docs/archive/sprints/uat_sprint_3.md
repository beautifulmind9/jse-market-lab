# Sprint 3 — UAT Notes

## Objective
Validate whether the guidance language feels natural, clear, and useful for Jamaican users.

## Test Focus
This sprint is focused on wording clarity, not new decision logic.

## User Types
- Everyday Jamaican investor
- Beginner investor
- More experienced investor

## Validation Questions
- Can someone read the guidance once and understand it immediately?
- Does the Clear version sound natural and familiar?
- Does any wording still sound foreign, too technical, or too formal?
- Do users understand what action they can take?
- Does the Pro version feel cleaner without becoming confusing?

## Scenarios
### High severity overlap
- Does the user understand that the price can move quickly around results?
- Does “put in a smaller amount” feel clearer than “reduce exposure”?

### Caution severity overlap
- Does the user understand that the price may be less steady for a while?
- Does “go in with less” or similar language feel natural?

### Info severity overlap
- Does the user understand that results are coming soon and may affect price?

## Observations
## Early Observations
- Clear mode is easier to understand at a glance
- Pro mode remains useful for more experienced users
- Planner-level mode selection creates a more consistent reading experience
- Further live user testing is still needed to refine wording

## Risks to Watch
- Guidance may still sound too formal
- Clear mode may become too wordy
- Pro mode may drift back into finance jargon
